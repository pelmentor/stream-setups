> **Measurement conditions:** active stream on both modems, no phone/tablet charging.

| Port | Load | Power (min – max) |
|---|---|---|

## 🔌 Power wiring

```
Wattico Airbank (27000 mAh ≈ 85 Wh)
├─ C1 ──PD 12V──► USB HUB ──┬──► M.2 adapter #1 + T77W968 + fan
│   (1.66–5.00 W)           ├──► M.2 adapter #2 + L850-GL + fan
│                           └──► (8 unused USB-A ports)
├─ C2 ──5V─────► UVC-HDMI Converter ──USB-A──► USB endoscope (PiP camera)
│   (1.61–1.94 W)
└─ A  ──5V─────► FEELWORLD CAMKOO H2U (PiP mixer)
    (2.85–2.93 W)

Anker 737 (24000 mAh ≈ 86 Wh, 140 W PD)
├─ C1 ──PD─────► Orange Pi 5 Plus (encode + SRT 12 Mbps)
│   (3.00–3.60 W)
└─ A  ──5V─────► Sony HDR-AS300 (live + NP-BX1 trickle charge)
    (1.70 W)
```

**Cables:**
- Wattico C1 → hub: bundled PD cable USB-C → DC barrel 12V (12V trigger)
- Wattico C2 → UVC-HDMI: regular USB-C ↔ USB-C (defaults to 5V)

## ⏱ Runtime (based on measurements)

| Battery | Usable capacity | Best case (min load) | Worst case (max load) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anker 737 | ~86 Wh | 4.70 W → **~18 h** | 5.30 W → **~16 h** |
| Wattico Airbank | ~85 Wh | 6.12 W → **~14 h** | 9.87 W → **~8.5 h** |

**Bottleneck:** Wattico → **8.5–14 hours of runtime for the whole setup**, depending on how hard the modems are pushing.

The Anker is loaded at ~4% of its 140 W PD ceiling — plenty of headroom for:
- charging a phone/tablet (~10–20 W),
- topping up the Wattico over PD if needed.

## 📦 Per-device (with measurements)

### 🍊 Orange Pi 5 Plus — **3.00–3.60 W** (Anker C1)
- H.265 encoding + SRT 12 Mbps — all of this is **handled by belabox firmware** (you only pick codec and bitrate in the web UI)
- Range driven by encoder activity and SRT network stack
- RK3588 hardware encoder handles this with negligible CPU load
- Huge PSU headroom (recommended 5V/4A = 20 W)

### 🎬 Sony HDR-AS300 — **1.70 W** (Anker USB-A)
- Live HDMI output + USB power running alongside the NP-BX1
- Stable 340 mA @ 5V — maintains NP-BX1 charge during shooting rather than fast-charging it
- Without USB the camera runs ~2 h on the internal battery

### 🔌 USB HUB ORICO USB 3.0 10 ports + 2 modems — **1.66–5.00 W @ 12V** (Wattico C1)
- Powered through the bundled PD cable USB-C → DC barrel 12V (with 12V trigger)
- Wattico C1 delivers 12V@2.5A = 30 W (×6 headroom even at peak)
- Current on the 12V input: 0.14–0.42 A
- **The most dynamic node** — peak 3× higher than idle due to modem TX bursts
  when pushing keyframes / reacting to SRT NAK
- The hub feeds: adapter #1 + T77W968, adapter #2 + L850-GL, fans

### 📺 UVC-HDMI Converter (U2H) + USB endoscope — **1.61–1.94 W** (Wattico C2)
- Powered on a dedicated port (NOT through the hub)
- U2H itself + USB endoscope on its USB-A input
- Small variation — depends on video stream activity

### 🎥 FEELWORLD CAMKOO H2U (PiP mixer) — **2.85–2.93 W** (Wattico USB-A)
- Accepts 2× HDMI (Sony AS300 + UVC-HDMI), outputs composite with a PiP window
- Power ~5V/0.57A — essentially stable (the mixer runs in a constant mode)

## 📝 Notes

- **Real numbers are 2–3× below datasheet estimates** — because the modems at 6 Mbps avg are far from their TX peak, the RK3588 hardware encoder offloads the CPU, and the PiP mixer is fairly simple.
- **Peak projection** (sharp TX spikes, bad cell coverage, both modems flat out): ~20–25 W on the Wattico. This is a **calculation**, not a measurement — but even then the output rails have ×3–4 headroom.
- **The USB-A output of the Wattico** (15 W max) is NOT suitable for the USB hub — the hub needs a 12V input.
- **L850-GL is sensitive to 5V sag** — a known weak spot of the modem, but in the current layout (short USB cables from modem to hub) it doesn't manifest under the 12 Mbps stream.
- Measurements were taken with a USB tester during an **active 12 Mbps stream** on both modems.
